Biography

Stephen Hawking (1942-2018) was a renowned British physicist known for his contributions to our understanding of black holes
and the nature of the universe. Despite being diagnosed with a debilitating motor neuron disease,
he made groundbreaking discoveries and became a symbol of scientific perseverance. Hawking's work on Hawking radiation and
his popular science books brought complex ideas to the masses, making him a beloved figure in both the scientific community and
popular culture.
